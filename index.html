8:45 AM - 10:30 AM: Hagia Sophia

Begin your exploration at this architectural masterpiece that has served as both church and mosque throughout history.

  • Entrance Fee: Free (functioning mosque)
  • Hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM (closed during prayer times)
  • Notes: Modest dress required; women need head covering

The Hagia Sophia was originally built as a cathedral in 537 AD under the Byzantine Empire. It later became a mosque after the Ottoman conquest of Constantinople in 1453, and in 2020 it was converted back to a mosque after serving decades as a museum.

Look for the famous Viking graffiti, magnificent dome, and beautiful Islamic calligraphy alongside Christian mosaics.

10:45 AM - 11:45 AM: Blue Mosque

A short 5-minute walk across the square from Hagia Sophia. Known for its stunning blue tiles and Ottoman architecture.

  • Entrance Fee: Free
  • Hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM (closed during prayer times)
  • Notes: Modest dress required; women need head covering

The Sultan Ahmed Mosque (Blue Mosque) was built between 1609 and 1616 during the rule of Ahmed I. It contains the tomb of its founder, and continues to function as a mosque today.

The mosque gets its nickname from the blue Iznik tiles adorning its interior walls. Notice the six minarets, unusual for Ottoman mosques which typically had two or four.

12:00 PM - 1:00 PM: Basilica Cistern

Visit this ancient underground water reservoir with its atmospheric lighting and famous Medusa head pillars.

  • Entrance Fee: 650-1000 TL (~$19-30)
  • Hours: 9:00 AM - 10:00 PM daily
  • Booking: Tickets available at entrance or online at yerebatan.com

The Basilica Cistern was built in the 6th century during the reign of Byzantine Emperor Justinian I. It's the largest surviving Byzantine cistern in Istanbul.

Don't miss the famous Medusa head columns at the northwest corner of the cistern. One head is upside down and the other is sideways, possibly because the builders repurposed materials from earlier Roman structures.

2:45 PM - 4:30 PM: Topkapi Palace

Spend your afternoon exploring this vast complex that served as the residence of Ottoman sultans for nearly 400 years.

  • Entrance Fee: 750 TL (~$22) + 750 TL (~$22) for Harem section
  • Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (closed Tuesdays)
  • Booking: Tickets at entrance or online at muzekart.com

Topkapi Palace was the primary residence of Ottoman sultans from 1465 to 1856. The palace complex consists of four main courtyards and numerous smaller buildings.

Highlights include the Imperial Treasury containing the 86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond, the Holy Relics section housing items believed to belong to Prophet Muhammad, and the famous palace kitchens.

The Harem (separate ticket) was the private quarters of the sultan and his family. It contains over 400 rooms, including the sultan's and queen mother's apartments.

4:45 PM - 6:30 PM: Grand Bazaar

One of the world's largest and oldest covered markets with over 4,000 shops. Explore the labyrinth of shops selling everything from spices to textiles, jewelry, and souvenirs.

  • Entrance Fee: Free
  • Hours: 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (closed Sundays)
  • Getting There: 10-minute walk from Topkapi Palace or T1 tram to Beyazit station

The Grand Bazaar dates back to 1455 and houses over 4,000 shops spread across 61 covered streets. It attracts between 250,000-400,000 visitors daily.

Popular items to shop for include Turkish carpets, ceramics, lamps, spices, leather goods, gold jewelry, and textile products. The bazaar is organized by product categories, with different sections dedicated to specific goods.

9:15 AM - 10:30 AM: Galata Tower

Start your day with panoramic views of Istanbul from this medieval stone tower, just a short walk from the cruise port.

  • Distance: 10-15 minute walk from cruise port
  • Entrance Fee: 1060 TL (~$32)
  • Hours: 8:30 AM - 10:00 PM daily
  • Booking: Tickets at entrance or online at muze.gov.tr

Galata Tower was built in 1348 during the Genoese period as part of the defense wall surrounding the Genoese colony of Galata. Standing at about 67 meters (220 ft) tall, it offers a 360-degree panoramic view of Istanbul.

The tower has served many purposes throughout history, including as a fire watchtower, an astronomical observatory, and now as a museum and tourist attraction.

2:30 PM - 3:15 PM: Taksim Square

Visit this major city square known for its monuments, including the Independence Monument honoring Ataturk, founder of modern Turkey.

  • Distance: 15-minute walk from Cicek Pasaji
  • Entrance Fee: Free
  • Highlights: Independence Monument, Republic Monument, cultural center

Taksim Square is a major transportation hub and the modern heart of Istanbul. The Republic Monument (1928) in the square commemorates the formation of the Turkish Republic in 1923.

The square is surrounded by restaurants, shops, and hotels, and marks the beginning of the famous Istiklal Street. It's both a popular tourist destination and a traditional gathering point for public celebrations, parades, and demonstrations.

Istanbul Transportation Guide

Istanbulkart

The essential public transportation card for Istanbul.

  • Card Cost: 130 TL (~$4)
  • Where to Buy: Terminals, major transit stops, convenience stores
  • Single Fare: 9.90 TL with card (25-30 TL without)
  • Transfer Discount: Yes, within 2 hours
  • Coverage: Trams, buses, metro, ferries, funicular

One card can be used for up to 5 people. Just tap multiple times for multiple passengers.

Travel Tips & Recommendations

Money & Payments

  • Currency: Turkish Lira (TL). $1 USD ‚âà 33 TL (May 2025)
  • ATMs: Widely available and often offer better rates than currency exchange offices
  • Credit Cards: Widely accepted in shops, restaurants, and attractions, but carry cash for small vendors
  • Tipping: 10-15% in restaurants; round up taxi fares
  • Haggling: Expected in bazaars and markets, but not in established shops

Notify your bank of your travel plans to avoid card blocks on foreign transactions.

Cultural Tips

  • Dress Code: Modest dress for mosques (covered shoulders/knees); women need head covering
  • Photography: Permitted in most places; avoid photographing military sites
  • Prayer Times: Mosques close to visitors during prayer (5 times daily)
  • Turkish Tea: Accepting tea when offered is a friendly gesture
  • Greeting: A simple "Merhaba" (Hello) is appreciated

Turkish people are known for their hospitality. Don't be surprised by friendly invitations or offers of assistance.

Time-Saving Tips

  • Museum Pass Istanbul: Consider purchasing if visiting multiple museums (2500 TL, ~$75)
  • Advance Tickets: Book online for Topkapi Palace and Basilica Cistern
  • Early Starts: Begin sightseeing early to avoid crowds
  • Visiting Order: This itinerary minimizes travel time between attractions
  • Cruise Return: Allow at least 60 minutes before departure

Security checks at major attractions can add wait time. Pack light and avoid bringing large bags.
